This example shows that the Gap feature as calculated is actually NOT a straightforward linear combination of the 
leftedge, rightedge and homologyLength. Actually, knowing the closest possible locations of the edges to the cutsite 
does not allow you to accurately calculate the gap. You can see this in the examples below. The main problem is that
when you have multiple options, you don't know which way the homology should go, and therefore it's hard to calculate 
a straightforward formula. You need to have non linear conditional checks etc.

Note there is no pattern for when the breaks are on either side of the cutsite indicating what correction to make, there's always a counter example
